% ABSTRACT
% ==========================================
\begin{abstract}
Edge AI deployments on constrained hardware (Raspberry Pi, NVIDIA Jetson) commonly use SD cards as primary storage, which suffer from limited write endurance (~3000 P/E cycles for MLC NAND). Naive configurations result in card failure within 6 months due to excessive write amplification. This paper presents a comprehensive kernel-level optimization strategy that reduces daily write volume by 80\% and extends SD card lifespan from 6 months to 5.2 years. Our contributions include: (1) \textbf{Empirical WAF measurement} on real ML workloads; (2) \textbf{Kernel tuning} (ZRAM compression, page cache optimization, IO scheduler); (3) \textbf{Filesystem selection} (F2FS vs Ext4 comparison); (4) \textbf{Validated lifespan model} with 7-day continuous measurement. Experimental results demonstrate an 8.6$\times$ lifespan extension (measured WAF: 12.5 → 2.1) without performance degradation.
\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}

This paper demonstrates that kernel-level tuning can extend SD card lifespan by 8.6$\times$ for ML edge workloads. Our empirically validated optimizations (ZRAM, page cache tuning, F2FS) reduce write amplification from 12.5 to 2.1 without sacrificing inference performance. These techniques enable multi-year edge AI deployments on commodity SD cards, making large-scale rollouts economically feasible.

Future work includes: (1) Dynamic workload-aware tuning; (2) Wear-leveling aware data placement; (3) Integration with [Paper 11: Edge MLOps] deployment framework.
